Chapter I — Prologue

The Last
Cartographer

In the age when the world still held its breath — when mountains whispered and oceans kept their secrets — there lived a woman who drew maps of places that did not yet exist. Her name was Seren. And one evening, a map arrived that she had not drawn.

II

Chapter II — The Journey

Crossing the
Fractured Lands

The map spoke of floating islands adrift above the Shattered Abyss — each holding a fragment of a dying sun. With only a lantern and her compass, Seren leapt between worlds, the void yawning wide beneath her boots. Each island whispered the same name: Aethon.

III

Chapter III — The Discovery

The City at the
Edge of Knowing

Aethon rose from the emerald mist like a cathedral made of starlight — a city that had mapped itself out of existence to survive the end. The inhabitants had become pure light. And at the city's heart, a single, fading flame: the Last Light that kept the world from forgetting itself.

IV

Chapter IV — The Danger

The World
Begins to Burn

But the flame had enemies — the Unmakers, ancient forces that had fed on the forgetting. They tore at the seams of Aethon, pulling the city back into the void. Columns cracked. Islands fell. The map in Seren's hands began to bleed. She had minutes. Maybe less.

V

Chapter V — Resolution

The Light
Remembered

Seren pressed her palms to the dying flame and spoke every name she had ever mapped — every river, every ruin, every road not yet travelled. The light surged. The constellations rewrote themselves. Aethon exhaled. The world remembered it existed, and that was enough to let it live.
