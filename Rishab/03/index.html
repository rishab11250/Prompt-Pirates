🇺🇦 UKRAINE — RUSSIA
Interstate Conflict · Invasion
CRITICAL

Russia's full-scale invasion began February 24, 2022 — the largest conventional war in Europe since World War II. Western intelligence estimates 250,000+ Russian combatant deaths; Ukraine has suffered ~100,000 combat losses and 15,000+ civilian deaths. Front lines in Donbas and Zaporizhzhia remain largely static. Russia controls approximately 20% of internationally recognised Ukrainian territory. 5M+ Ukrainians live as refugees; 4M are internally displaced.

🇸🇩 SUDAN
Civil War · SAF vs RSF
CRITICAL

War between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and Rapid Support Forces (RSF) erupted April 2023. ACLED estimates 20,373 killed between Aug 2024 – Aug 2025 alone — likely an undercount. Over 10 million people have been displaced, making it the world's largest displacement crisis. Famine declared in Darfur. El-Fasher remains besieged. UN classifies this as the world's worst humanitarian emergency, surpassing even Gaza in displacement scale.

Chronology of Conflict

CRISIS TIMELINE 2021–2026

Key events that shaped the current global conflict landscape.

August 2021
Taliban seizes Kabul — US withdrawal from Afghanistan
After 20 years of US military presence, Taliban forces swept into Kabul within days. 176,000+ had died in the conflict since 2001. The international community scrambled to evacuate civilians. ISIS-K bombed Kabul airport killing 170+ people.
February 2022
Russia launches full-scale invasion of Ukraine
On February 24, 2022, Russia invaded Ukraine with over 190,000 troops — the largest military offensive in Europe since WWII. NATO imposed sweeping sanctions. The conflict would go on to produce 500,000+ casualties and 5M+ Ukrainian refugees.
April 2023
Sudan erupts — SAF vs RSF war begins
Fighting broke out between Sudan's armed forces and the Rapid Support Forces paramilitary. Within weeks, Khartoum became a battleground. 10M+ people have since been displaced in what the UN calls the world's worst humanitarian crisis.
October 7, 2023
Hamas attacks Israel — Middle East war erupts
Hamas launched an unprecedented attack on Israel, killing 1,200 people and taking 250 hostages. Israel's military response in Gaza has killed 40,000+ Palestinians. The conflict expanded into Lebanon, Yemen, Syria, Iraq, and ultimately Iran.
November 2024
Ceasefire talks stall — global conflict count hits record 56
Uppsala Conflict Data Program confirms 56 active armed conflicts — the highest count since records began. Diplomatic efforts in multiple theatres collapse. ACLED confirms 240,000+ deaths in 12 months across global conflict zones.
December 2024
Assad falls — Syria enters post-war transition
After 13 years of war and with Russian support diminished, rebel forces captured Damascus in days. Bashar al-Assad fled to Russia. The fall of Assad ends one chapter but opens another: factional fighting, ISIS resurgence, and Turkish-Kurdish tensions continue.
Early 2025
Sudan famine declared — DRC M23 offensive intensifies
UN formally declares famine in Darfur, Sudan — the first since 2011 in Somalia. Simultaneously, M23 rebels backed by Rwanda capture key DRC cities. Mineral-rich eastern Congo becomes a flashpoint for regional proxy warfare.
Mid 2025
US–Israel operations target Iran — regional war escalates
Direct US-Israel military operations against Iranian nuclear and military infrastructure mark a dramatic escalation. Iran responds with ballistic missile strikes. The region teeters on the edge of wider conflagration as diplomacy collapses.
March 2026
46 active armed conflicts — world reaches highest point since WWII
As of March 2026, the Global Conflict Map tracks 46 active armed conflicts across 76 countries. Ukraine's front lines remain static. Sudan's crisis deepens. Gaza ceasefire negotiations continue without resolution. The global conflict landscape remains at its most complex since the post-WWII era.

RESOURCES & ORGANISATIONS

Key organisations monitoring conflict, providing humanitarian aid, and advocating for peace. Data sources used in this platform are listed below.

Data Source
ACLED — Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project
Real-time data on political violence and protests worldwide. Primary source for conflict event counts and casualty statistics on this platform.
Data Source
Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP)
Sweden-based program providing the world's most reliable and long-running data on organised violence and armed conflict since 1946.
UN Agency
UNHCR — UN Refugee Agency
Tracks and protects the world's forcibly displaced populations. Manages refugee registration and coordinates humanitarian response in over 130 countries.
UN Agency
UN OCHA — Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs
Coordinates global emergency humanitarian response. Publishes the Global Humanitarian Overview and tracks funding gaps across all major crises.
NGO — Conflict Analysis
International Crisis Group (ICG)
Independent organisation conducting field research on conflicts worldwide. Provides policy recommendations to governments and international organisations to prevent and resolve conflict.
NGO — Humanitarian
Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF)
Provides emergency medical aid in conflict zones worldwide including Sudan, Gaza, DRC, Yemen, and Myanmar. Operates in areas where access is most dangerous.
International Law
ICRC — International Committee of the Red Cross
The ICRC protects civilians and prisoners of war under international humanitarian law. Monitors compliance with the Geneva Conventions in active conflict zones.
Peace Research
Institute for Economics & Peace — Global Peace Index
Publishes the annual Global Peace Index measuring the state of peace across 163 countries. 2025 index shows global peacefulness at its lowest point since 2008.
Journalism
Bellingcat — Open Source Intelligence
Investigative journalism using open-source intelligence to verify conflict events, war crimes, and military movements. Has documented conflicts in Ukraine, Syria, and beyond.
